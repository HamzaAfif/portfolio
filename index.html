My Story & Vision

Returning to Morocco after my time in Ukraine wasn’t just a change of place—it was a turning point in my life. I had been pursuing a degree in pharmacy when circumstances beyond my control forced me to leave everything behind and start over. That experience shaped me in ways I didn’t expect, teaching me resilience and fueling my determination to channel my passion for technology into something meaningful. I’ve always been the person people come to for help with tech problems, but this chapter of my life gave me the chance to take that passion further, turning it into a career where I could build tools and solutions that make a difference.

Reading a resume, a portfolio, or even this section might not be enough to truly stand out—because on paper, everyone looks good. What really defines a person isn’t just their achievements or how they present themselves, but the challenges they’ve faced, the problems they’ve solved, and the resilience they’ve shown along the way. For me, what keeps me going, even after setbacks and failures, is the way I see every situation as an opportunity to learn and grow. There’s always light at the end of the tunnel, and it’s worth pushing through to reach it.

I knew I didn’t see the world the same way as others during a moment in class. A teacher asked, "If you had 30 million euros, would you stop working and learning?" While everyone else eagerly said yes, I couldn’t help but think differently. To me, that kind of opportunity wouldn’t mean stopping—it would mean doing more, learning more, and exploring even further. That’s the mindset I carry with me: every problem is a chance to grow, every failure is a lesson, and every success is just another step on the journey. It’s this perspective that keeps me grounded and driven to create solutions that truly matter.
